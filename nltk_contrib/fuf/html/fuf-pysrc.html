 1  """ 
 2  FUF is an interpreter, originally written by Michael Elhadad in Common Lisp  
 3  for a functional unification based language specifically designed 
 4  for the development of text generation applications. 
 5  This module is a port of that interpreter that relies upon the  
 6  C{nltk.featstruct.FeatStruct} to acheive the same task.  
 7   
 8  Unifier 
 9  ======= 
10   
11  C{fuf.Unifier} is the implementation of the FUF 
12  unification algorithm that (currently) inludes 
13  the processing of most (but not all) of the features defined 
14  in the original Common LISP version. 
15   
16  Link Resolution 
17  --------------- 
18   
19  FUF grammars allow for addressing parts of the grammar through 
20  absolute and relative links. The targets of the links  
21  can be resolved by using the C{link.LinkResolver} class. 
22   
23   
24  Type Definitions 
25  ---------------- 
26   
27  FUF grammars allow for the specification of feature 
28  value taxonomies. The value types can by stored and used 
29  by the C{fstypes.FeatureTypeTable} and C{fstypes.TypedFeatureValue} 
30   
31   
32  Linearization 
33  ------------- 
34   
35  The linearization of the successful unification is perfomed by the 
36  C{linearize} module. 
37   
38   
39  Morphology 
40  ---------- 
41   
42  The morphology module is currently B{not implemented}. 
43   
44  Converter 
45  ========= 
46   
47  C{fufconvert} defines several functions for converting 
48  FUF grammar files from their original s-expression 
49  syntax to C{nltk.featstruct.FeatStruct}. 
50  """
